<p><p>Conjugate Vaccines are classified into two main types: Monovalent Conjugate Vaccines and Multivalent Conjugate Vaccines. Monovalent Conjugate Vaccines target a single specific pathogen or antigen and stimulate an immune response against it. On the other hand, Multivalent Conjugate Vaccines are designed to protect against multiple pathogens or antigens, providing broader immunity. Both types of vaccines use a conjugate, which is a combination of a weak antigen and a strong antigen, to enhance the immune response and improve vaccine effectiveness.</p></p>
