<p><p>Commercial biotechnology separation systems are used to separate and purify different components in biotechnology processes. These systems comprise various types of technologies, including membrane filtration, liquid chromatography, centrifuge, electrophoresis, and flow cytometry. Membrane filtration involves the use of a porous membrane to separate particles based on size. Liquid chromatography separates components using a stationary phase and a mobile phase. Centrifuge separates components based on density using spinning. Electrophoresis separates molecules based on their electrical charge and size. Flow cytometry uses lasers to analyze and sort cells based on their physical and chemical characteristics.</p></p>
