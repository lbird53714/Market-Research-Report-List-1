Type II CPPs, on the other hand, are produced through acid hydrolysis, which involves the use of acid to break down casein. Both Type I and Type II CPPs have various applications in the food and beverage industry, including improving calcium absorption, enhancing nutritional value, and promoting gut health.</p></p>
