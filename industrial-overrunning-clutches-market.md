<p><p>Industrial overrunning clutches are devices used in machinery to transmit torque in one direction while allowing free rotation in the opposite direction. The sprag clutch type consists of a series of cylindrical sprags that engage and disengage with the inner and outer races, allowing power transmission in one direction. Spring clutches, on the other hand, use a set of springs to engage and disengage the clutch components. Both types are commonly used in various industrial applications where torque needs to be transmitted efficiently and securely.</p></p>
