<p><p>An armoured thermocouple is a type of temperature sensor that consists of two different metal wires, known as thermoelements, that are welded together at the measurement end. These thermoelements generate an electromotive force that is proportional to the temperature difference between the measurement end and the reference end. The measurement end is usually encased in a metallic sheath for protection and durability.</p><p>The armoured thermocouple market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.5% during the forecast period. The market is primarily driven by the increasing demand for temperature sensors in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, oil and gas, and healthcare. These industries require accurate temperature measurement for process control, quality assurance, and safety purposes.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a trend towards miniaturization and integration of temperature sensing technologies. This trend is driven by the need for compact and lightweight sensors in applications such as wearable devices, drones, and IoT devices. Manufacturers are developing armoured thermocouples with smaller sizes and higher levels of integration to meet these demands.</p><p>Another trend observed in the armoured thermocouple market is the growing adoption of wireless temperature monitoring systems. These systems eliminate the need for cumbersome wired connections and allow for remote monitoring of temperature data. They are especially useful in applications where accessing the measurement point is challenging or hazardous.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in materials and manufacturing technologies have led to the development of armoured thermocouples with enhanced performance characteristics such as improved accuracy, high reliability, and resistance to harsh environmental conditions. These developments are expected to drive the market growth during the forecast period.</p><p>Overall, the armoured thermocouple market is poised for steady growth due to the increasing demand for temperature sensors in various industries, the trend towards miniaturization and integration, the adoption of wireless temperature monitoring systems, and advancements in materials and manufacturing technologies.</p></p>
