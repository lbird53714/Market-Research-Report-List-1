<p><p>Pu'er tea is divided into two main market types: Pu'er raw tea and Pu'er cooked tea. Pu'er raw tea, also known as Sheng or Green Pu'er, is made by sun-drying the leaves and allowing them to age naturally over time. This type has a more vibrant and astringent flavor profile. Pu'er cooked tea, also known as Shou or Black Pu'er, undergoes an accelerated fermentation process, resulting in a darker and earthier taste. This type has a mellow and smooth flavor, with a unique aroma. Both types of Pu'er tea have their own dedicated consumer base and market demand.</p></p>
