<p><p>The commercial aircraft engine market comprises four main types: turboprop, turbofan, turboshaft, and piston engines. Turboprop engines use a gas turbine to drive a propeller and are commonly used in smaller regional aircraft. Turbofan engines utilize an additional fan to produce extra thrust and are the most popular choice for commercial airliners. Turboshaft engines power helicopters and other rotorcraft, providing high power output for vertical takeoff and landing operations. Lastly, piston engines are typically found in smaller general aviation aircraft, using reciprocating pistons to generate thrust.</p></p>
