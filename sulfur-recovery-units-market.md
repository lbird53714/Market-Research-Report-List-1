<p><p>Sulfur Recovery Units (SRUs) are used in oil refineries and natural gas processing facilities to convert hydrogen sulfide (H2S) into elemental sulfur. The market for SRUs can be divided into three main types. The first is the Claus Process, which is the most commonly used method. It involves converting H2S into elemental sulfur through a series of reactions. The second type is the Tail Gas Treatment, which further reduces sulfur emissions by treating the tail gas from the Claus Process. Lastly, there are other types of SRUs that utilize different technologies or combinations of processes to recover sulfur.</p></p>
