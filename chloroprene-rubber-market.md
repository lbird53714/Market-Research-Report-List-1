<p><p>Chloroprene rubber, also known as polychloroprene or Neoprene, is a type of synthetic rubber that is widely used in various industries due to its excellent resistance to heat, chemicals, and abrasion. The increasing demand for chloroprene rubber from end-use industries such as automotive, construction, electrical, and manufacturing is driving the market growth. On the basis of product type, the market is categorized into adhesive grade and general grade. Adhesive grade chloroprene rubber is extensively used in the automotive industry for applications such as hoses, belts, seals, and gaskets. General grade chloroprene rubber finds applications in various industries including wire and cable, construction, and coatings.</p><p>As for the latest trends, there is a growing focus on developing bio-based alternatives to chloroprene rubber to reduce the environmental impact.
