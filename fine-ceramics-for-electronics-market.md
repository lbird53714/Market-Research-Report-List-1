<p><p>The market for fine ceramics in electronics can be classified into three types: ferroelectric, piezoelectric, and pyroelectric. Ferroelectric ceramics possess a unique spontaneous polarization, allowing them to be electrically switched. Piezoelectric ceramics can convert mechanical stress into electrical voltage and vice versa, making them suitable for sensors and actuators. Pyroelectric ceramics can generate an electrical charge when exposed to temperature variations, which is used in motion sensors. These ceramics play a crucial role in electronics, enabling various applications such as memory devices, sensors, and thermal detectors.</p></p>
