<p><p>The transportation ticketing smart card market can be classified into two main types: contact and contactless. Contact smart cards require physical insertion into a card reader for data transfer. This technology is commonly used in integrated ticketing systems. On the other hand, contactless smart cards use radio frequency identification (RFID) technology, enabling data transfer without physical contact. They are widely employed in transportation ticketing systems due to their convenience and rapid processing. Both types play a crucial role in facilitating seamless and efficient travel experiences for commuters.</p></p>
