<p><p>The wood-derived food additives market offers a range of different types of additives, including fibrillated cellulose, lignin, xylan, and vanillin. Lignin is another wood-derived additive that can be used as a natural antioxidant or a stabilizer. Xylan, also derived from wood, is often used as a food emulsifier or a gelling agent. Vanillin, derived from the lignin in wood, is a popular flavoring agent in the food industry.</p></p>
