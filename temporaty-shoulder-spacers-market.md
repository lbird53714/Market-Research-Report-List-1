<p><p>Temporary shoulder spacers are devices used in shoulder arthroplasty procedures to provide stability and maintain joint space prior to the placement of a permanent implant. There are two main types of shoulder spacers available on the market: cementless and cemented. Cementless spacers rely on biological fixation through bone ingrowth, allowing for long-term stability and reducing the risk of peri-prosthetic infection. On the other hand, cemented spacers utilize bone cement to secure the device in place, ensuring immediate stability but potentially increasing the risk of infection or loosening over time.</p></p>
