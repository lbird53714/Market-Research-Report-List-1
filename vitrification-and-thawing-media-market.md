<p><p>Vitrification and thawing media are specialized solutions used in cryopreservation techniques to protect biological samples, such as embryos, oocytes, and sperm, during freezing and thawing processes. Vitrification is a method that allows samples to be cooled rapidly, forming a glass-like state, and preventing the formation of ice crystals that can damage cells. Thawing media, on the other hand, are used for the rehydration and recovery of the samples during the warming process.</p><p>The vitrification and thawing media market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
