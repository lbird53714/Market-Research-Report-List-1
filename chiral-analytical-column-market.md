<p><p>Chiral analytical columns are used in chromatography to separate and analyze chiral compounds. Several types of chiral columns exist in the market, each with its unique characteristics. Cellulose-based columns separate enantiomers based on the interaction between cellulose and the compounds. Cyclodextrin columns utilize the chiral cavities of cyclodextrins to separate enantiomers. Macrocyclic antibiotics chiral columns are coated with antibiotics, which interact with enantiomers to achieve separation. Protein-based columns separate enantiomers based on interactions with proteins. Ligand exchange columns use ligands with chiral selectivity to separate enantiomers. Prikle columns are formed by derivatizing silica particles with chiral reagents. Lastly, other types of chiral columns may also exist in the market with varying separation mechanisms.</p></p>
