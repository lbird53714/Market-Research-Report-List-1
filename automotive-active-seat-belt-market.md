<p><p>The automotive active seat belt market includes various types of seat belts designed to ensure passenger safety. Two-point belts are simple lap belts that secure the passenger at the hips. Three-point belts add an additional shoulder strap to provide better restraint. Four-point belts incorporate an extra strap that crosses over the passenger's chest. Five-point belts include two additional straps that secure the passenger's legs. Six-point belts add another strap that attaches the shoulder and lap belts to the car's frame. Seven-point belts are similar to six-point belts but also feature an anti-submarine strap that prevents passengers from sliding under the belt in the event of a collision.</p></p>
