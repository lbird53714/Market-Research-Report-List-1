<p><p>Bronchopulmonary dysplasia (BPD) is a chronic lung disease that primarily affects premature infants who required mechanical ventilation and oxygen therapy. It is characterized by inflammation and scarring in the lungs, leading to impaired development and function. The treatment for BPD includes supportive care such as respiratory support, nutrition management, and medications.</p><p>Bronchopulmonary dysplasia drugs are primarily aimed at reducing inflammation and promoting lung development in affected infants. Corticosteroids, such as dexamethasone and hydrocortisone, are commonly used to reduce inflammation in the lungs. Additionally, bronchodilators such as albuterol may be prescribed to open up the airways. However, the specific drugs used for BPD treatment may vary depending on the severity of the condition and the patient's response to therapy.</p><p>The future outlook of the Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia Drug Market appears to be promising.
