<p><p>Bipolar membrane electrodialysis (BMED) is an advanced membrane separation technology that utilizes bipolar membranes to carry out electrochemical reactions and separate ions from a solution. The process involves the use of cation and anion exchange membranes, sandwiching a bipolar membrane between them. This setup facilitates an electrochemical reaction at the bipolar membrane interface, which generates acid and alkali on either side of the membrane. This technology is widely used in various industries such as water treatment, chemical processing, food and beverage, and pharmaceuticals.</p><p>The future outlook of the bipolar membrane electrodialysis market is promising, with an expected growth rate of 9.6% during the forecasted period. The market for this technology can be categorized into two types: Two-compartment and Three-compartment. In the Two-compartment market, there are two compartments or chambers, one for the anode and one for the cathode, separated by a bipolar membrane. This setup enables the selective passage of ions through the membrane. In the Three-compartment market, an additional compartment is added between the anode and cathode compartments, which further enhances the separation of electrolytes and improves the efficiency of the process.</p></p>
