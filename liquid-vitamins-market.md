<p><p>The liquid vitamins market can be categorized into two types based on their solubility: water-soluble and fat-soluble vitamins. Water-soluble vitamins, such as vitamin C and B-complex vitamins, easily dissolve in water and are not stored in the body, requiring daily intake. On the other hand, fat-soluble vitamins, including vitamins A, D, E, and K, are stored in the body's fat tissues and can be stored for longer periods. Understanding this distinction is crucial for consumers to meet their nutritional needs and ensure the right dosage of vitamins.</p></p>
