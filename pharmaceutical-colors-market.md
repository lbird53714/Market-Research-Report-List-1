<p><p>Pharmaceutical colors are categorized into three types: synthetic, natural, and natural-identical. Synthetic colors are chemically produced and provide a wide range of vibrant hues. Natural colors are derived from plants, minerals, or other natural sources, offering a more organic option. Natural-identical colors are produced through chemical synthesis but mimic the natural colorants found in nature. These types cater to different preferences and requirements, allowing pharmaceutical companies to choose the most suitable colors for their products while considering factors like safety, stability, and desired visual appeal.</p></p>
