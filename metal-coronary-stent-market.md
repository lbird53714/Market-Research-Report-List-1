<p><p>A metal coronary stent is a small mesh-like tube made from metals such as stainless steel or cobalt-chromium alloy. It is used to treat atherosclerosis by expanding and restoring blood flow in narrowed or blocked coronary arteries. The stent is inserted into the artery and then expanded using a balloon catheter, creating a scaffold that helps to maintain the artery's patency.</p><p>The metal coronary stent market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, driven by the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and growing adoption of minimally invasive procedures. According to a market analysis, the global metal coronary stent market was valued at $7.41 billion in 2020 and is expected to reach $15.12 billion by 2028, with a CAGR of 12.2% during the forecast period.</p><p>Factors contributing to the market's growth include advancements in stent technologies, such as drug-eluting stents which release drugs to prevent restenosis, and bioresorbable stents that gradually dissolve over time. These innovations have improved the efficacy and safety of the stents, leading to increased adoption worldwide.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing aging population, sedentary lifestyles, and unhealthy dietary habits have resulted in a higher incidence of coronary artery diseases, further driving the demand for metal coronary stents. Moreover, the market is also benefiting from the growing healthcare infrastructure and investments in emerging economies.</p><p>However, challenges such as the high cost of stents and the risk of complications associated with their usage may hinder market growth. Regulatory reforms and reimbursement policies have also impacted the market dynamics in certain regions.</p><p>In conclusion, the metal coronary stent market is experiencing robust growth, fueled by technological advancements, increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, and favorable demographic trends.
