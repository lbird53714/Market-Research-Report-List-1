<p><p>The bioplastic rigid packaging market is segmented into two types: Bio-PET and Bio-PP. Bio-PET stands for bio-based polyethylene terephthalate, which is a type of plastic derived from renewable sources like sugarcane. It is commonly used in the production of bottles, trays, and clamshells for packaging purposes. On the other hand, Bio-PP refers to bio-based polypropylene, another type of bioplastic derived from renewable feedstocks. It is typically used in the manufacturing of rigid packaging materials such as containers, caps, and closures. Both Bio-PET and Bio-PP are sustainable alternatives to traditional petroleum-based plastics.</p></p>
