<p><p>Orphan lung diseases refer to a group of rare respiratory conditions that affect a small number of individuals. These diseases can be genetic, autoimmune, or acquired, and often have no known cure. Medications such as corticosteroids, immunosuppressants, and bronchodilators are commonly used to alleviate symptoms and slow down the progression of the disease. Oxygen therapy is also prescribed to patients with severe respiratory insufficiency.</p><p>The future outlook of the orphan lung diseases treatment market is promising.
