<p><ul><li>Non-Electrically Conductive</li><li>Electrically Conductive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The adhesive for electronics market includes two main types: non-electrically conductive and electrically conductive adhesives. Non-electrically conductive adhesives are designed to provide strong bonding for electronic components without causing electrical conductivity. They are commonly used for structural applications, such as adhering circuit boards. On the other hand, electrically conductive adhesives are formulated to create a conductive path between components, allowing for the transmission of electrical signals. They are often used in applications where electrical conductivity is required, such as attaching surface-mounted devices.</p></p>
