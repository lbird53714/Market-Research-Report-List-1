<p><p>Wet tissue and wipe market types can be classified based on the technology used to manufacture them. Spunlace technology involves the use of high-pressure water jets to entangle fibers, resulting in a strong and absorbent fabric. Airlaid technology uses a non-woven process that combines air and fibers to create a bulky and soft material. Wetlaid technology utilizes a papermaking technique to produce non-woven fabrics with good absorbency. Spunlaid technology involves the extrusion of fibers onto a moving web to create a non-woven material with good strength and durability. These different technologies cater to varying consumer needs and preferences in the wet tissue and wipe market.</p></p>
