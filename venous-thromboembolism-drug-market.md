<p><p>The venous thromboembolism drug market comprises various types of medications. Heparin is a commonly used anticoagulant that prevents blood clotting. Apixaban, Dabigatran, Rivaroxaban, and Edaxaban are newer anticoagulant drugs that work by inhibiting certain clotting factors in the blood. Warfarin, an older anticoagulant, reduces the blood's ability to clot by targeting vitamin K. All these drugs are used to treat and prevent blood clots in conditions such as deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism, but they differ in terms of their mechanism of action and dosing regimens.</p></p>
