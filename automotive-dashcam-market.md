<p><p>An automotive dashcam, also known as a dashboard camera, is a small video camera mounted on the dashboard or windshield of a vehicle. AI-based dashcams can analyze real-time footage to detect objects, pedestrians, and lane departures, providing additional safety features. Cloud connectivity enables automatic video uploads, remote viewing, and data storage, enhancing convenience and accessibility for users.</p><p>Furthermore, there is a rising demand for dual-channel dashcams that can record both the front and rear views simultaneously. These dual- or multi-lens cameras are particularly useful for taxi drivers, rideshare drivers, and fleet managers as they provide comprehensive coverage and evidence in case of accidents or disputes.</p><p>In conclusion, the automotive dashcam market is expected to witness steady growth due to increasing safety concerns and the need for surveillance systems in vehicles. <p><p>The automotive dashcam market can be divided into two types: integrated and portable. Integrated dashcams are designed to be permanently installed in the vehicle and are typically integrated into the rearview mirror or dashboard. They provide a seamless and discreet video recording solution. On the other hand, portable dashcams are designed to be easily moved between vehicles. They often come with suction cup mounts and can be attached or detached as needed. Portable dashcams offer flexibility and convenience for users who frequently switch vehicles or prefer a more versatile recording option.</p></p>
