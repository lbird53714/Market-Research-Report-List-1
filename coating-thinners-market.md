<p><p>Coating thinners are widely used in various industries to thin or dissolve paints, varnishes, and coatings. The market for coating thinners can be classified into different types. Mineral spirits are a type of coating thinner derived from petroleum and commonly used due to their low odor and fast evaporation rate. Turpentine is another natural solvent extracted from trees, often used in oil-based paints and varnishes. Naphtha is a fast-drying and highly volatile solvent. Toluene is a powerful solvent with a strong odor, mainly used in industrial coatings. Methyl Ethyl Ketone (MEK) is a versatile solvent that is effective in dissolving many types of coatings. Dimethylformamide (DMF) is a highly effective solvent, often used in the production of coatings. 2-Butoxyethanol is a water-soluble solvent that can be used in a variety of coatings. Finally, "Others" encompass other types of coating thinners available in the market.</p></p>
