<p><strong>The In Line Process Viscometer (ILPV) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Rotational</li><li>Torsional Oscillation</li><li>Vibration</li><li>Moving Piston</li><li>Coriolis</li><li>Dynamic Fluid Pressure</li><li>Acoustic Wave (Solid State)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The In Line Process Viscometer (ILPV) market is segmented into different types based on their working principles. </p><p>Rotational viscometers measure the viscosity of fluids by rotating a spindle or rotor. Torsional oscillation viscometers apply an oscillatory motion to a liquid and measure the resulting torque. Vibration viscometers determine viscosity by measuring the damping effect on an oscillating element. Moving piston viscometers use the displacement of a piston to measure viscosity. Coriolis viscometers exploit the inertia of fluids to calculate viscosity. Dynamic fluid pressure viscometers measure the pressure drop across a constriction. Acoustic wave viscometers utilize solid-state technology to measure viscosity using sound waves.</p></p>
