<p><p>Digital-to-Analog Converters (DAC) are electronic devices that convert digital signals to analog signals. There are several types of DACs available in the market. The R-2R DAC uses a resistor network, while the String DAC uses a ladder network of resistors. High-Speed Current-Steering DACs employ fast switching currents for accurate signal conversions. Delta-Sigma DACs use a one-bit digital signal and oversampling to achieve high resolution. Other DAC technologies include segmented DAC, successive approximation DAC, and flash DAC. These different types of DACs cater to various applications and requirements in the electronics market.</p></p>
