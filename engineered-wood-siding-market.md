<p><p>Engineered wood siding is a type of exterior cladding made from wood fibers and resin, designed to enhance durability and reduce maintenance. There are three main types: Oriented Strand Board (OSB), High-density Fiberboard (HDF), and Veneered Plywood. OSB consists of layers of wood strands pressed together, providing strength and rigidity. HDF is made from fine wood fibers compressed under high pressure, resulting in a smooth and dense surface. Veneered plywood is constructed by layering thin wood veneers on top of one another, providing an attractive appearance and structural integrity.</p></p>
